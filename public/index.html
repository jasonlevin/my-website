About me



I am a research coordinator at the University of Georgia's Clinical Affective Neuroscience lab (PI: Dr. Gregory Strauss), which focuses on the negative symptoms of schizophrenia. I grew up in the suburbs of Chicago and graduated from Northwestern University in 2020 majoring in neuroscience and minoring in psychology. I am an aspiring physician passionate about mental health looking to enroll in medical school in fall 2022.

In high school and college, I was heavily involved in policy debate as both a competitor and judge. I enjoy biking, listening to music, and Chicago sports teams.
